Episode 19

Monday

Today is a pretty ordinary day. I go to work, I go home, and I chill. Thats about that

Tuesday

Today after work I go to play squash over at the Mission Rec center for the first time. I had never played squash before but one of my friends was thinking about it so I said I was down to play. For those who have never played squash before its a racquet sport kinda like tennis or badminton except instead of hitting over a net instead both sides hit towards a wall and once the ball hits the wall the other side hits it back.

Playing squash was super fun even if I was pretty bad at it. I certainly didn’t realize how much wrist strength it required to hit the ball back each time. While there we found a couple other folks who invited us to a squash group chat so I may be playing more squash in the not too distant future

Wednesday

Today after work I go to run club. The run was fun but not particularly noteworthy. After the run I meet a guy who apparently has 50 pairs of shoes. I gotta admit this was definitely unusual.

Thursday

Today at work I’m talking about run club with a coworker of mine and she asked me if I’m in the marina run club and I said no. I asked if she was and she said “I’m not that single … yet”. It took me a moment to recognize the implication she thought I was *that* single but perhaps given that I told her about the posters perhaps that was obvious.

After work I go to bar night. This bar is great because it let me blatantly eat my Mixt salad there which is always appreciated. While at the bar we meet a couple of interesting characters. Some people who we suspect are a bit less than homed. One of whom bears a striking resemblance to a friend of mine there and tries to get me to invite her to karaoke. In theory I’m not opposed to inviting people I just met that day to do karaoke with me but in practice the people I invite should be a bit less sketchy than she was. The other person asked for investment advice, got defensive, and left to go look for a “golden shovel” in the streets. Theres probably a lesson in there somewhere

Friday

Today after work one of my friends has a sea shanty party. Think of it like group karaoke with songs where nobody really takes the lead and everybody sings in union to very repeatable and easy to remember lyrics. This taught me something important about myself that I’m not the biggest shanty fan. I liked the company of course but I can’t say shanties are necessarily my favorite songs.

During the party I ask for a spoon to eat some guacamole and the host jokingly gives me a straw. The expectation would naturally be that I ask for / get a spoon instead but I took it as a challenge and ate guacamole out of a straw. It went better than I thought. Not easily mind you but still more manageable than I thought.

I duck out of this party for a while and go to my coworkers visa celebration party. She recently got her visa updated so she can continue being here which is awesome! I go to her party only to find that I’m the first person there and that they weren’t expecting me to come so early (8 minutes past the start time on partiful). I forgot that most parties operate that way. I spend quite a bit of time talking with her boyfriend and one of his friends and the boyfriend in particular proves to be a troll on about my level which is a pretty rare find.

Somehow the topic of work gets brought up and I mention that by default I assume he’s a software engineer. He then tries to sell me a bunch of spiels about how he is actually a marketer, nay a soap opera actor. Its quite hilarious and refreshing to meet someone who responds to my trolling in a similar style.

We then spend a lot of time getting into Latin American geography and I learn some interesting factoids like that Nicaragua is roughly 10x the size of El Salvador.

Around this time some of their other friends come to the party. At this point we’ve reached the always fun situation where everyone their knows each other and I’m the only one who doesn’t. They talk a lot about different people who aren’t here and since I don’t know who these people are I’m forced to come up with my own headcannon. They mention someone named Rhythm who I’ll assume was named because the moment he was born he could immediately play the drums and his parents wanted to lock in his destiny. They also mentioned someone named Yash who I assume likes cats. I don’t really have something specific to point to as to why but more so a subtext thing.

After this some of my coworkers come and I finally feel like I know people again. Interestingly at this point the room splits with me and my coworkers on one half and boyfriend and his friends in the other half. At this point I go back to the shanties.

By the time I come back to the shanties the main shantying is done and I chill and talk with people there for a bit. It seems like they’re planning to go out afterwards but I’m too tired so I just hit the bed

Saturday & Sunday

You might be wondering why I put Saturday and Sunday in one heading here … well we’ll get to that later.

Saturday starts off as usual with trash pickup. Today the automatic route assigning system I mentioned a few issues back actually works without hitch. I get some requests for feedback for a next version which I may or may not implement.

After trash pickup I go to put up v2 of my date me posters. Interestingly enough at the same time one of the trash pickup organizers is putting up fliers for an upcoming trash pickup so we wind up doing this together. Having another person was pretty useful as we were both doing a lot of the same basic stuff so working together we could streamline the process.

From there I get ready for the powerpoint party I’m hosting. In case anyone hasn’t been (and made the poor choice not to go to this one 🙄) a powerpoint party is a party where everyone makes a powerpoint on a topic they are passionate about and presents it in front of the group. I added a twist where everyone presents someone elses powerpoint which proved to be quite fun. We had quite the unique array of presentations including

A pasta tier list

A guide on what makes good flag design

Citing the cryptid known as “Humza IqBALL”

Writing a weekly newsletter

Cool flags around the world (yup two separate people made presentations on flags :D)

A “plowerpoint” on the restaurant Plow

A presentation that truly can’t be contained by such things as a summary title

How to speak Cantonese like a local (naturally I made sure not to give another Chinese person this one)

It was really fun seeing all presentations! There was a great synergy where I swapped both the flag people and had them present each others presentations. After that we played a bunch of Secret Hitler and Chameleon.

From there two of my other friends came over to start our next big thing which is a 45 mile walk around the city. Yeah you heard that right the original plan was to do this

Starting at around 10PM saturday night and go through the night til Sunday and finish the walk. I was the one who originally came up with the idea and roped my two friends into this only to find that at around 9:30 I was getting pretty sleepy.

Before we leave I get some feedback that perhaps going through Bayview in the late night is not the best idea so I decide to change the route. How? Well we will get to that.

Also they brought a bunch of supplies like food and water while I didn’t really pack much for this walk. And by much I mean anything.

Maybe in retrospect I should have naped throughout the day and not hosted a party before going off on this walk but ya know it is what it is. Eventually my two friends are able to drag me from my house and after getting some provisions from a Mexican place and a corner store we set off.

What winds up happening is a pretty complicated series of events so we’ll break this up into specific story arcs.

Overcoming fatigue

We start off with what winds up being either the hardest or second hardest part of this walk for me which is overcoming my fatigue. We start off at Lands End and make our way through it over to the presidio and down to Golden Gate park

I struggle to keep up with my friends and their rambunctious energy. One of them jokes that this is a field trip and they’re young children with lots of energy and I’m the reluctant dad along for the ride.

Towards the end of this stage I reach one of my lower points. I want to use the restroom and since its close enough by I lead the group so that we go towards my apartment so I can use the restroom there. Once they realize what I’m doing they basically tell me thats not really in the sprit of such walks and after conceding I use “the nature” instead. Somehow at this point I magically start feeling more awake and we enter stage 2 of the walk

Hills, hills everywhere

As I mentioned earlier the original route I had planned would have us go through Hunter’s point at about 2AM and after consulting with the people at Powerpoint party I decide to make a strategic pivot. The goal is to try to get to 45 miles but using a different more freeform route. I decide it may be fun to have us go through a whole bunch of hills at once so I take us on a route that goes through

Tank Hill

Twin Peaks

Bernal Heights

Holly Park

Billy Goat Hill

Glen Canyon

Mt. Davison (tallest natural point in the city)

Throughout this phase I start gaining more energy and things become a lot more fun! At one point we found a 24/7 donut store at 2AM that sold Ito Ens and I got all the Ito Ens they had. It may have only been 3 but I got them. The Ito Ens undoubtedly played a vital role in keeping me up.

We go through all sorts of different views which are quite nice but as one of my friends noted tend to look relatively similar early in the day (or very late in the day I suppose).

After getting to Mt. Davison the sun started coming up and things became much brighter. We watch the sunrise over at Mt. Davidson. After the sunrise the lack of sleep catches up with my friends. At this point I’m not really sure what I should do. Initially I think that I’m going to bow out but then I decide to go to a couple more hills. In the back of my mind I think that maybe I can still get to 45 miles. Originally my goal was to just do Granview and Corona heights and call it there since the weather wasn’t even good anyway.

Starting off strong solo

After parting ways with my friends I decide to go up north to Grandview park and then go over to Buena Vista and Corona heights

I’m still feeling quite strong at this point and quite enjoying everything so far

A trashy detour

Since I’m the only one doing the walk I decide to take a detour and go to trash pickup. When I get to Mannys I decide to pace around for a while to keep my mileage going up even while I was at the cafe. For once I’m actually thankful that trash pickup was a relatively small crew today as I don’t feel the pressure to a whole bunch of trash friends. The friends who are there are for the most part going to Dolores park but since its 420 a bunch of people would be smoking there which I would hate anyway so I continue on

Embarcadero

After trash pickup the sun comes out and I have a renewed motivation. I walk from Mannys over to the Embarcadero and then along the Embarcadero to Coit Tower. This is a really nice part of the walk as the weather is great and my motivation is still strong!

After Coit Tower I go down through Fishermans Wharf and the Marina. While at the Marina I check my Strava and realize that its seriously off.I realized later its because I accidentally paused it and didn’t resume it. Thankfully my health app is still a reliable mileage indicator.

Where it stops being fun

Once I get to the Marina the walk really starts taking its toll on me. At this point I’m roughly 34 miles in and I definitely am not enjoying it as much as I was at the beginning. I’m still able to keep going but the spark I had earlier was lost.

The last really nice view I had on this walk was here by Inspiration point. I walk up from the Marina over to the Presidio and get to Golden Gate Park which brings us to …

The final 5 miles

At this point in the walk I am pretty ready to be done. I’m still going only out of a desire to finish. I’ve come this far and I want to meet my original goal. Around here I think the mental fatigue really takes its toll as well. Its not that I’m tired (aka from the lack of sleep), moreso just the desire to talk with someone. I now understand how powerful having people cheer you on can be when running a marathon or something like that. I always used to think “why do people need to sign up for these fancy races to run when they can just run a marathon around them”, but I totally understand how that can be helpful towards the end. Thankfully I do have a groupchat I’m texting and their encouragement is of course greatly appreciated

I was hoping I’d run into somebody, anybody that I happened to know in GGP. And surprisingly enough I did. I ran into a bunch of people I knew from highschool over there. This was in the pre newsletter era but I did know a bunch of them lived in SF and I did talk with them before so it wasn’t totally unexpected but still definitely a nice surprise. Having someone to talk to did definitely help. I only walked with them for like a quarter of a mile or so as they needed to leave the park but it did give me just enough to see it through.

For the final part of the walk I walk across GGP and walk back. Never has a walk through GGP felt like such a slog to me but at this point this is how it is. I try my best to finish. I need to take more breaks to make sure I don’t overdo it with my legs. But somehow against all odds I get through GGP, go to Gus’s to get some berries, and as I leave Gus’s the walk finishes.

As soon as I finish I send this to the aforementioned group chat

At 5:11 PM after about 19 hours the walk comes to an end.

In total the walk was pretty much exactly 45 miles
