Tab 1

Hey friends! As mentioned here's the weekly newsletter that I promised! I have no idea if this will meet your expectations or not. All I can say is I tried my best to capture what I thought were the most interesting events this week. This week was decidedly more introspective than most for reasons I’ll get to.

Monday

The week started off pretty normal. We had an “offsite” for our team which took place in the company office. The beginning of the “offsite” had a Q/A session where I asked what the difference between an “offsite” and an “onsite” was. The head of the team said he wasn’t quite sure and everyone laughed. Other than that, nothing unusual happened at work. After work we had a company alumni reunion where I got to meet up with some of my former coworkers. It was great seeing folks again. What made it funny though was the bar we had this was also hosting a founder / vc meetup which resulted in a bunch of people interrupting us asking if we were founders. At one point I said “YES!”. I then launched my startup. I’ll share my pitch here

Do you or anyone you know have a nut allergy? Nuts are very healthy and a great source of protein. Lacking access can lead to huge “nut inequality”. Imagine further you’re a vegan, what are you gonna do? With the power of generative ai and deep neural networks we can make a new hypo-allergenic nut. This will be revolutionary! I call it bofa.ai

At this point you might be wondering why bofa? The answer is bofa deez nuts! . Needless to say they weren’t exactly impressed.

Tuesday

The workday proceeds pretty normal right until about 3PM when I decide to step out of the office for a walk. On the way I see a homeless person threatening to kill a couple. I’m far enough away and in the opposite direction from him that I’m not threatened but I couldn’t decide what to do. There was a part of me that wanted to intervene but ultimately I decided not to. The couple wound up going inside a coffee shop so they made it out OK. I still felt pretty bad for doing nothing, I texted some friends and the consensus was not intervening was the right call. One of them brought up the idea that I could have called the police but unfortunately this didn’t come til later after I forgot most of what the guy looked like so I couldn’t give a good description.

After this I had improv class. It was pretty good, I learned more about how to make different scenes connected to each other as if they lived in the same universe. Its part of the overall class which is about how to not waste ideas in scenes (think diving into a couple ideas deeply rather than meandering through a whole bunch of ideas by just scratching the surface). I hadn’t done improv in a few weeks and unfortunately it showed. I wasn’t at the top of my game but thankfully by the end I got some of the juices flowing and had a scene that got some big laughs.

Wednesday

Here too work was pretty ordinary with nothing particularly interesting to report. After work I had a reunion with a bunch of folks from a club I did in college. The club brought together both current members and alumni. It was great seeing a bunch of my old friends again, definitely crazy to see a bunch of people I hadn’t thought about in years. Amusingly enough one of the people there was a member who wasn’t super active in college and I hamfistedly asked out on a date many years ago. Looking back on that is like one of those memories that just reflexively makes you groan in embarrassment many years after the fact. My toes are curling now as I type this sentence. One other thing I tried to do at the reunion was convince a bunch of folks there that I was a new member, unfortunately nobody believed me :( . The most surreal part of the reunion was talking to folks who are currently freshman in college; between that and the 10 year high school reunion I had over thanksgiving I felt quite old indeed.

After the reunion I went over to a bar to meetup with some of my friends from run club. I was going to go to run club but decided to go to the reunion instead. Thankfully the home skillets were still there and we hung out for a while before heading home.

Thursday

The work day was mostly ordinary except for the fact that I noticed my manager scheduled a 15 minute 1:1 out of the blue. This was pretty odd as normally a 1:1 was 30 minutes which told me this wouldn’t be an ordinary meeting. I was half worried I was gonna get reprimanded over something I said (I do tend to make a lot of jokes some of which poke fun at the company, not maliciously mind you but I always worry that someone will think so), or that somehow I was getting fired or something. This doesn’t make a lot of sense considering my manager was praising me a week ago and telling me I was an integral part of the project but I still wasn’t completely at ease. I got to the 1:1 only to find out that I’m getting a bonus as part of my work on the aforementioned project. He mentioned something about this before and I was relieved that it finally happened! After that I had an interview with a company that reached out to me. I’m not really serious about this company but figured interview practice is always a good thing. It went fine, it was mainly the sort of interview where I talk about some of my past projects, nothing too involved.

After work I got dinner with the runner friends from yesterday. Good food and good company, not much more to say here.

Friday

I’ll preface this by saying Friday was the most emotional day for reasons you will see shortly.

Work itself was again ordinary. I leave a bit early because I need to prepare for a pasta night I am hosting. When I get home I get a text from someone telling me that a former OpenAI employee turned whistleblower was found dead in his apartment. As a note the phrase “OpenAI employee turned whistleblower” might raise some questions. I’m not going to speculate about any fowlplay that happened, thats not the point of why I’m bringing this up. This really shook me because I knew people who knew this person from college. Additionally he was 2 years younger than me at 26. I was really saddened to hear this and felt distraught. The first thing I did when finding out about this was calling my Mom and telling her how thankful I am that she raised me and that I appreciate her. One thing which I didn’t mention that happened at the reunion on Wednesday was that I met someone who worked at another big LLM company called Anthropic. He told me how hes been on medical leave for 2 months because of some surgery. I didn’t pry into details but one thing that both experiences taught me is that you never truly know someones life.

I have many flaws big and small but one that I feel particularly ashamed about is my tendency to be jealous of others who have things that I want, in this case whats perhaps the most high paying / prestigious job in tech. However both of these things made me realize that I should be very grateful for all the blessings that I have in my life. I have good health, I have an excellent support network of friends and family, and I’m financially independent with lots of money to save. Thats why I wanted to call my Mom to let her know how grateful I am.

Saturday

I wake up shortly before the Tornado warning. I look it and I’m quite flummoxed. The Bay Area is not Tornado alley after all and I would expect to see an Earthquake warning before a tornado warning. I’ll admit that after the Tsunami warning and all the flood warnings I was skpetical. That being said I did have a window open and could definitely feel some of the wind. On top of that there was that part of me in the back of my head that was wondering if it was real after all. Instead of doing anything practical I just stare at the clock and wait it out. As 6 o’clock came the wind started to die down and my skepticism increased. Eventually 6:15 rolled around and it looks like no Tornado after all!

A few hours later I go to trash pickup. While theres no tornado its still somewhat windy and rainy. Its trash pickups like this that make me wonder if there will ever be a trash pickup where its only me. Thankfully there were a few other people crazy enough to be there with me and we have a good time!

Later I go to Santacon. Some of my run club friends made a plan to go and I decided to tag along. I didn’t buy a ticket before hand considering the fact that I don’t drink and thought I could still tag along to bars along with the fact that a ticket was $52. When I walked into the first bar I was immediately struck with a sense of just how cramped this affair would be. I understand that bars aren’t always the best when it comes to space but this took it to a whole new level. I really had to fight just to move an inch. While it didn’t start off on the best foot once I was inside hanging out with my friends things went ok. After the first bar we go to another and unfortunately a problem ensues. It turns out that if I don’t have a ticket I needed to pay cover! And this cover was $30 mind you! Since thats tantamount to highway robbery I decided not to go and instead get dinner and walk around. It was certainly quite the juxtaposition seeing the sober Humza with all the drunken Santa Clauses wandering around.

At this point I was thinking more about all the things I wrote that I’m grateful for with y’all in the Friday section above. Another thing I thought about how was when I was younger I used to look down more on people who drank and partied and realized it was a defense mechanism because I never really understood them and was a bit scared that I didn’t . One goal of mine is to try and understand more perspectives and views. Mind you I’m not saying I need to agree with a whole bunch of views but more that I wish to understand them. I’m not talking politically either I mean more common things you see every day. Seeing people party in cramped bars is one example, another would be pets. Unlike almost everyone I know I don’t really care much for pets. I also don’t really understand the way people go all gaga for cats and dogs, I’ve tried it a few times but it feels incredibly forced to me. It almost makes me wonder if I’m really human or if there is a critical part of the human condition I’m missing out on.

After that bit of soul searching and some great Japanese curry courtesy of Nippon curry I meet back up with my friends after they leave the bar and we walk around a bit and get in line at another bar. Unfortunately there too I learn the cover is $10 without a ticket. While more manageable I still don’t wanna pay so I continue walking around. Eventually I find my way back to the bar and it turns out one of my friends needed to leave so I was able to get his ticket! Success! I went into the bar cover free and met back up with my other friends. After this we go to another bar.

While waiting in line I see a full on fight break out! It started when I see some woman fall down. I attempt to try and help her up but then I see more people fall down around her and I run out of the way. Afterwards I see a quarrel between two Santas where they are straight up punching each other. I watch from the sidelines until it breaks up. One of the bystanders winds up loosing her phone. I ask if she needs someone to call it but they turn down the offer. One of my friends helps up the woman who fell. I’ll admit I was shaken up by this; while I heard of these things happening at Santa con before seeing it happen made me quite upset. I’m all for having a good time but this is too much and frankly unacceptable behavior. Once that dies down and we wait in line we get into the next bar. Its here unfortunately that I see a huge swarm of people and I begin to feel quite uncomfortable. I wouldn’t say I’m claustrophobic but having to fight so much to move so little really annoyed me. After spending a bit of time here I decided to bid Santacon 2024 fair well. All in all I’d say I give it a C+, a bit above average but ultimately still pretty mid. Just not for me unfortunately.

At this point I get home and start typing up the letter! Not much else to say I suppose, thanks for taking the time to read!
