A weekly newsletter from Humza Iqbal.

Monday

Today is a pretty chill day. Work is normal and then I go home and chill. The fact that I can’t remember anything as of this Sunday is a sign that it was likely a day without much note.

Tuesday

Today I officially gave my 2 week notice at my job with my last day being Feb 14 (yes I am breaking up with my current job on Valentines day). You may recall from two newsletters ago that I recently did an onsite for a job I was interested in. I got the offer and I realized I wanted to take it. In case anyone is interested, the new place I”m going to is a startup called latenthealth. I’m not going to go through the full set of reasons I decided to go somewhere else but I wanted to highlight some of the most important here

I felt like I wasn’t really growing anymore in the job I was in. The work isn’t quite what I want to do and I think if I want to grow I need to change

This new company is doing something in the healthcare space that I think can tangibly and positively help real people. I’ve spent a lot of time working at B2B startups whose main customers are big enterprise companies. You could make the argument that helping big companies like banks and what not could have downstream effects that help society but it doesn’t feel the same to me tangibly. I want something with a more immediate and positive effect

This new company is completely in person. I know many folks like remote work for the flexibility that it offers but I do think that in person is a massive advantage for doing good work. I’ve definitely had more interesting spontaneous discussions while in person. As I mentioned above I want somewhere to help me grow more and I think that in person will also help in addition to more interesting work

I’ve been at my current job for 3.5 years and wanted a change

Since some people who I’ve told about this have asked, $$ wasn’t really a big motivation for me one way or the other. I’m getting paid about the same, perhaps I could have tried to push for more $$ but I didn’t really think it was worth it to be honest.

I told my manager at work and he was very supportive. When I laid out my reasons for wanting to leave he completely understood and wished me good luck.

Wednesday

Today at work my skip level and I had a 1:1 where she tried to convince me to stay at the company. She asked if there was anything that would convince me to stay like more $$ or a different team. I laid out the reasons I wanted to leave and she was also quite supportive and said that what I was laying out was both very fair and mature. I suppose if I was $$ maxing I could have tried to use this as leverage to see if they would get me to pay more but since I didn’t care I didn’t really feel like trying to waste anyone's time. With that said though she was interested in seeing if I could push out my departure date in exchange for some more money. She said she would get back to me on exact numbers but I told her that I likely wouldn’t be interested but would hear her out.

After work I go to Midnight runners. Like last week I capitalize on the fact that if I take it chill I can have a good conversation with friends, get my steps in, and actually have fun as opposed to being out of breath after every stop. This run was only 3 miles as opposed to the usual 5 but that suits me just fine. We then hang out at Underdogs as per usual.

I still told her no and she was cool with it and didn’t press me on it.

Now I start the process of figuring out who is going to take over my projects. Coordination is always fun … NOT!

Work is overall normal today, twinged with a bit of bittersweetness.

After work I hang out with a friend. We go to play pinball over at Free Gold Watch. I was a bit perplexed at pinball because I didn’t really understand how I can actively improve at the game. From my perspective you just hit the flippers whenever the ball comes and usually if I fail its because the ball falls in a way that I can’t hit it. My friend who is more of a pinball aficionado mentions that if you stick to a given machine long enough you learn how it works and where the ball tends to fall.

As we play I do begin to learn a bit about the pinball machines I tend to like. I like the ones that light up a lot with fun animations as opposed to some of the more poorly lit ones. From there we go to Beit Rima and catch up more over dinner.

Over the dinner I learn that my newsletter pays dividends as he asks me about stuff I mentioned that I wouldn’t have thought to bring up since it happened a few weeks ago. Success! During dinner a bit of a slack emergency happens and he captures this simply beautiful picture of me
